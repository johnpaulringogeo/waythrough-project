Section 8 Waiting List Preferences: How to Move Up the List

Matt · December 9, 2026 · 7 min read

Section 8 waiting lists can take years, but many PHAs give priority to applicants who meet certain criteria — called "preferences." Common preferences include: being homeless, living in substandard housing, paying more than 50% of income toward rent, being a veteran, being a victim of domestic violence, and being involuntarily displaced. Preferences can move you from a 5-year wait to months. Each PHA sets its own preference categories.

How Preferences Work

When a PHA pulls names from the waiting list, applicants with preferences are selected before those without — regardless of when they applied. Think of it as two separate lines: the preference line moves first. Within the preference group, order is usually determined by date and time of application.

Some PHAs use a point system where different preferences are worth different amounts. An applicant with multiple preferences (e.g., homeless AND veteran) would score higher than someone with just one.

Common Preference Categories

Homelessness — currently without a fixed, regular, and adequate nighttime residence. This often requires documentation from a shelter, social worker, or other service provider. Severe rent burden — paying more than 50% of income toward rent. Involuntary displacement — lost housing due to disaster, government action, domestic violence, or no-fault eviction. Veterans — many PHAs give preference to veterans and their families. Local residency — living or working within the PHA's jurisdiction. Domestic violence — fleeing or recently fled domestic violence. Elderly or disabled — age 62+ or having a qualifying disability. Currently in substandard housing — living in conditions that don't meet HQS.

How to Claim a Preference

When you apply (or when the PHA contacts you from the waiting list), you'll be asked about preference categories. Be thorough — don't skip any that might apply. You'll need documentation: a letter from a shelter or homeless services provider, your current lease and rent receipts showing rent burden, a police report or domestic violence service letter, DD-214 for veteran status, pay stubs showing income relative to rent, or medical documentation for disability.

If your circumstances change while you're on the waiting list (you become homeless, experience domestic violence, etc.), contact the PHA and update your application with the new preference information.

Finding PHAs With Favorable Preferences

Since every PHA sets its own preferences, it's worth checking multiple PHAs in your area. One might have a veteran preference that another doesn't. Check each PHA's Administrative Plan (available on their website or by request) for their full list of preference categories. Apply to every PHA where you qualify for a preference — this maximizes your chances.

What Preferences Won't Do

Preferences move you up the list, but they don't guarantee immediate housing. If many applicants have the same preference, there's still a wait within that group. Preferences also don't override income eligibility or other program requirements. And they can't help if the waiting list is closed — you still need to apply during an open enrollment period.
