Section 8 Portability: How to Move Your Voucher to Another City

Matt · October 21, 2026 · 8 min read

Section 8 vouchers are portable — you can use them anywhere in the country where a PHA administers the program. This is called "porting" your voucher. The process involves your current PHA (the "initial PHA") coordinating with the PHA in your new area (the "receiving PHA"). It typically takes 2-4 weeks for the paperwork, plus your normal housing search time. There are a few restrictions and some common complications.

How Portability Works

When you want to move to an area administered by a different PHA: notify your current PHA in writing that you intend to port your voucher. Your current PHA contacts the receiving PHA and sends your paperwork (the portability packet). The receiving PHA can either "absorb" your voucher into their program or "bill" your original PHA for your subsidy. You search for housing in the new area using the receiving PHA's payment standards and voucher size.

The receiving PHA becomes your point of contact for everything — inspections, recertifications, landlord issues — once you move.

Absorption vs. Billing

This is important because it affects your subsidy amount. If the receiving PHA absorbs you, your voucher size and payment standard change to match their program. This can help or hurt you depending on the local market. If they bill your original PHA instead, your original PHA's payment standard may still apply (though this varies). Most PHAs prefer to absorb when they have available vouchers.

You generally have no control over which option the receiving PHA chooses.

When You Can Port

New voucher holders must typically use their voucher in the issuing PHA's jurisdiction for the first year (the "initial lease-up" period). After that, you can port anywhere. Exceptions: you can port immediately if you were living in the receiving PHA's area when you applied, or if you're moving to escape domestic violence, or other approved reasons.

If you already have a lease with your voucher, check your PHA's policy on when you can give notice and port. Most require 30-day written notice.

Common Portability Problems

Paperwork delays between PHAs — the initial PHA takes weeks to send the portability packet. Different payment standards — the new area's payment standard may be lower, meaning you can afford less. Different voucher sizes — the receiving PHA may assign a different bedroom size. Different utility allowances — this changes your out-of-pocket costs. The receiving PHA may have a slower inspection process.

The biggest risk: higher-cost areas may make your voucher worth less than it was in your original location. Run the numbers before committing to the move.

Tips for a Smooth Port

Start the process early — don't wait until your current lease is ending. Get the receiving PHA's payment standards in advance so you know what you can afford. Ask your current PHA about their portability processing timeline. Keep copies of every document you submit. Follow up weekly with both PHAs during the transfer. Have a backup plan in case the port takes longer than expected.
