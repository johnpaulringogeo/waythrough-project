Does Section 8 Check Your Credit Score?

Matt · November 4, 2026 · 6 min read

PHAs do not check your credit score when determining eligibility for Section 8. The voucher program is based on income, not creditworthiness. However, individual landlords can and often do run credit checks as part of their tenant screening — and bad credit is one of the most common reasons voucher holders get rejected by landlords. The key distinction: the PHA qualifies you for the voucher, but the landlord decides whether to rent to you.

What PHAs Actually Look At

When you apply for Section 8, your PHA evaluates: household income (must be below income limits), household composition, citizenship or eligible immigration status, criminal history (varies by PHA — usually limited to drug-related and violent felonies), and whether you owe a debt to any PHA. That's it. No credit check.

If you owe money to a previous PHA (from an overpayment or damage claim), that can disqualify you until the debt is repaid or a repayment agreement is in place.

What Landlords Look At

Landlords can screen voucher holders using the same criteria they apply to market-rate tenants. This typically includes: credit score and credit history, rental history (evictions, late payments, landlord references), criminal background, income verification (your portion of rent vs. your income), and employment history.

A credit score below 580-600 often triggers automatic rejection from corporate landlords and property management companies. Individual landlords tend to be more flexible.

Working Around Bad Credit

Focus on smaller landlords who own one or two properties — they're more likely to consider your whole story rather than just a number. Offer a larger security deposit if your state allows it. Provide reference letters from previous landlords, employers, or caseworkers. Bring a letter from your PHA confirming your voucher and the guaranteed rent payment. Explain your credit situation honestly — medical debt, divorce, and past financial crises are understandable to many landlords.

Some landlords will accept a co-signer. Others will overlook bad credit if your rental history is clean (no evictions, no landlord debt).

Improving Your Credit While on Section 8

Being on Section 8 is actually a great time to work on your credit because your housing costs are stabilized. Get your free annual credit reports from annualcreditreport.com and dispute any errors. Pay all bills on time going forward — payment history is 35% of your score. If you have outstanding debts, negotiate payment plans or settlements. Consider a secured credit card to build positive history.

If you're enrolled in FSS (Family Self-Sufficiency), your coordinator may offer credit counseling and financial literacy workshops as part of your plan.
