How Section 8 Annual Recertification Works

Matt · October 7, 2026 · 7 min read

Annual recertification is a yearly review that your PHA conducts to verify your continued eligibility for Section 8 and recalculate your rent portion. You'll need to report your current income, household composition, and assets. Missing your recertification can result in termination of your voucher — so take it seriously. Most PHAs send a notice 60-90 days before your recertification date.

What Gets Reviewed

During recertification, your PHA looks at: all income for everyone in your household (wages, benefits, child support, self-employment), your household composition (who lives with you — has anyone moved in or out?), your assets (bank accounts, vehicles, property), and your continued eligibility for the program.

Based on this review, your rent portion — your Total Tenant Payment — gets recalculated. If your income went up, your rent goes up. If it went down, your rent goes down. The adjustment takes effect on your anniversary date.

Documents to Gather

Start collecting these before your appointment: pay stubs for the last 60 days for every working household member, benefit award letters (SSI, SSDI, TANF, unemployment), bank statements for the last 3-6 months, tax returns (if filed recently), child support documentation, self-employment records if applicable, IDs for all household members, and Social Security cards.

The exact list varies by PHA, but having all of this ready prevents delays. Your PHA will also run third-party income verification through HUD's Enterprise Income Verification (EIV) system, so be honest — discrepancies get flagged.

The Recertification Process

Typically: your PHA sends a recertification packet in the mail 60-90 days before your anniversary date. You complete the forms and gather your documents. You attend an in-person or phone appointment (some PHAs now allow online submissions). Your caseworker reviews everything, verifies income, and calculates your new rent portion. You receive a notice of your new rent amount, effective on your anniversary date.

The whole process should take 2-4 weeks from your appointment, but busy PHAs can take longer.

What Happens If You Miss It

Missing your recertification is one of the most common reasons people lose their vouchers. If you don't respond to the initial notice, most PHAs send a second notice. If you still don't respond, they issue a termination notice. Once that happens, you have a right to an informal hearing to explain why you missed it — but there's no guarantee you'll keep your voucher.

If you're going to miss your appointment for a legitimate reason (medical emergency, family crisis), contact your PHA immediately and ask to reschedule. Being proactive goes a long way.

Tips for a Smooth Recertification

Keep a folder of your important documents year-round so you're not scrambling. Report income changes as they happen during the year (interim recertifications) so there are no surprises at annual. Respond to your PHA's notice within a week of receiving it. Bring extra copies of everything to your appointment. Ask questions if you don't understand your new rent calculation.
