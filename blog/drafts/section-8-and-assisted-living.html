Can You Use Section 8 for Assisted Living or Senior Housing?

Matt · November 11, 2026 · 7 min read

Section 8 vouchers can be used for senior-designated housing and independent living apartments, but generally cannot be used for traditional assisted living facilities or nursing homes. The key distinction is whether the housing is a "dwelling unit" (covered by Section 8) versus a "medical facility" (not covered). However, some PHAs participate in special programs that bridge the gap, and other federal programs specifically serve elderly residents who need supportive services.

What Section 8 Covers for Seniors

Your Housing Choice Voucher works in: regular apartments (including senior-only buildings that restrict tenants to age 55+ or 62+), subsidized senior housing complexes, independent living communities, and shared housing arrangements. The voucher pays the rent portion, and you pay 30% of your adjusted income — the same as any other voucher holder.

Many seniors on Section 8 live in buildings specifically designed for elderly residents — these often have accessibility features, community spaces, and sometimes voluntary service coordination.

Why Assisted Living Is Different

Traditional assisted living facilities provide personal care services (help with bathing, dressing, medication management) bundled with housing. Section 8 vouchers are designed to pay for housing only — not personal care. Since assisted living fees are a combination of rent and services, the voucher program generally doesn't apply.

However, there are workarounds. Some assisted living facilities will separate the housing cost from the service cost, allowing the voucher to cover the housing portion while Medicaid or other programs cover the services. This arrangement is rare but does exist in some states.

Programs Specifically for Elderly Housing

Section 202 — HUD's Supportive Housing for the Elderly program provides subsidized housing for people age 62+. These buildings offer below-market rent and often include service coordinators who help connect residents to community resources. Section 202 properties don't require a voucher — they have their own subsidy.

PACE (Program of All-Inclusive Care for the Elderly) — for those who qualify for nursing home care but want to remain in the community. PACE provides all medical and supportive services and can work alongside Section 8.

Medicaid Home and Community-Based Services (HCBS) waivers — these fund in-home care services that can make it possible to age in place while using Section 8 for housing.

Reasonable Accommodations for Elderly Tenants

If you're an elderly Section 8 participant with disabilities, you have the right to request reasonable accommodations. This might include: a ground-floor unit, grab bars in the bathroom, a unit near transportation or medical facilities, a live-in aide (whose income doesn't count toward your household income), or exceptions to pet policies for emotional support or service animals.

Planning Ahead

If you're a senior on Section 8 and anticipate needing more support in the future, start planning now. Contact your local Area Agency on Aging (find them at eldercare.acl.gov or call 1-800-677-1116). They can connect you to services that help you stay independent longer while keeping your voucher.
