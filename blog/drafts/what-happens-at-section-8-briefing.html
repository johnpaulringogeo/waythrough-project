What Happens at Your Section 8 Briefing (And How to Prepare)

Matt · October 28, 2026 · 7 min read

The Section 8 briefing is a mandatory orientation that happens after you're selected from the waiting list but before you receive your voucher. It's where your PHA explains the program rules, gives you your voucher (with its dollar amount and bedroom size), and starts the clock on your housing search. Briefings typically last 1-3 hours and attendance is required — missing it usually means losing your spot.

What Happens During the Briefing

The briefing is essentially Section 8 boot camp. A PHA staff member explains: how the voucher program works, your payment standard (the maximum the PHA will pay toward rent), your bedroom size (based on household composition), your family's portion of rent (30% of adjusted income), the housing search timeline (usually 60-120 days), what kind of units you can rent, landlord and tenant responsibilities, and inspection requirements.

You'll receive a packet of documents including your actual voucher, a list of participating landlords (if available), Request for Tenancy Approval (RFTA) forms, and program rules.

What to Bring

Your PHA's briefing notice should list what to bring, but generally: government-issued photo ID for all adult household members, Social Security cards for all household members, birth certificates for children, proof of income (recent pay stubs, benefit letters, tax returns), bank statements, and your invitation letter. Bring originals and copies of everything. Some PHAs require specific forms to be completed before the briefing.

Critical Decisions at the Briefing

Pay close attention to your payment standard — this determines how much you can spend on rent. If you find a unit that costs more than the payment standard, you pay the difference out of pocket (up to a limit — your total rent can't exceed 40% of your adjusted income at initial lease-up).

Also note your voucher's expiration date. You have a limited time to find housing, typically 60-120 days depending on the PHA. Mark this date immediately. You can request extensions, but they aren't guaranteed.

Questions to Ask

Can I port my voucher to a different area? What's the process for requesting a voucher extension? How do I submit a Request for Tenancy Approval? What happens if a unit fails inspection? Can I rent from a family member? How do I report income changes? Who is my caseworker and how do I contact them?

Write your caseworker's name and direct phone number down before you leave. This person is your lifeline for the next several months.

After the Briefing

The clock starts now. Begin your housing search immediately — don't wait. Read through your entire packet that evening. Start calling landlords the next day. The biggest mistake new voucher holders make is waiting too long and running out of time on their voucher.
