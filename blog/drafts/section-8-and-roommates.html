Can You Have a Roommate on Section 8?

Matt · October 14, 2026 · 6 min read

Yes, you can have a roommate on Section 8 — but they must be approved by your PHA and listed on your voucher as a household member. An unauthorized occupant (someone living with you who isn't on your voucher) is a serious program violation that can result in termination. Any adult who stays overnight regularly — generally more than a few nights per month — may be considered a household member whose income must be reported.

The Rules About Who Lives With You

On Section 8, every person living in your unit must be listed on your HUD-50058 form. This includes: all family members, any romantic partners who stay regularly, adult children who move back in, and roommates. Before anyone moves in, you must request approval from your PHA. The PHA will screen the new household member (background check, income verification) and recalculate your rent portion based on the new household income.

This matters because Section 8 calculates your rent based on total household income. Adding someone with income increases the household's Total Tenant Payment.

What Counts as "Living With You"

PHAs define a household member as anyone who uses your unit as their primary residence. The common guideline: if someone stays overnight more than 3-4 nights per week on a regular basis, they're considered a resident. This applies to romantic partners, friends, and family members.

Guests are different — occasional visitors who stay a few nights are fine. But extended stays (usually defined as more than 14 consecutive days or a certain number of nights per year) require notification to the PHA.

The Danger of Unreported Household Members

This is where people get into serious trouble. If your PHA discovers someone is living with you who isn't on your voucher, you can face: termination of your voucher, a demand to repay past subsidy (because your rent was calculated without that person's income), and in rare cases, fraud charges.

PHAs discover unreported residents through: annual inspections, neighbor complaints, EIV system cross-checks, and utility usage patterns. It's not worth the risk.

How to Add Someone Properly

Contact your PHA and request to add a household member. You'll need: the person's ID, Social Security number, date of birth, income documentation, and consent for a background check. The PHA will process the request, which typically takes 2-4 weeks. Once approved, your rent gets recalculated.

If the person has a criminal background that doesn't meet PHA screening criteria, the request may be denied. You can request an informal hearing if you disagree with the denial.

Shared Housing and Split Vouchers

Some areas allow "shared housing" arrangements where two separate voucher holders share a unit. Each person's voucher covers their portion. This is relatively rare but becoming more common as housing costs rise. Ask your PHA if they allow shared housing arrangements.
