Indiana Housing Resources & Tenant Rights

Waythrough Project · Updated May 2026

Indiana sets its rental rules at the state level under Indiana Code Title 32, Article 31, and tenant protections are limited. There is no rent control, and the state has preempted local source-of-income ordinances — so a city like Indianapolis cannot require landlords to accept Housing Choice Vouchers. Deposits have no cap but must be returned within 45 days, and nonpayment gets a 10-day notice. The Indiana Housing and Community Development Authority (IHCDA) is the state housing agency. This page covers the statewide rules, the eviction timeline, and links to every Indiana city we cover.

Public Housing & Vouchers in Indiana

Section 8 Housing Choice Vouchers and public housing are run by local authorities — the Indianapolis Housing Agency, the Fort Wayne Housing Authority, and others. IHCDA administers vouchers in parts of the state and allocates Low-Income Housing Tax Credits — search HUD’s LIHTC database or read how to find LIHTC housing.

Apply to several authorities at once. Use HUD’s PHA directory or our how to find your PHA and how to apply for Section 8 guides. City waitlist status is on the city pages below.

Rent Control & Source-of-Income Preemption

Indiana has no rent control — there is no statewide cap and no city has one. The state also preempts local source-of-income protections: under Indiana Code 36-1-3-8.5 a city, county, or township cannot adopt an ordinance that would require a landlord to participate in Section 8, and a 2021 law (House Bill 1541) further barred localities from regulating most aspects of the landlord-tenant relationship. The practical result is that a landlord can legally decline a Housing Choice Voucher across the state. Part of using a voucher in Indiana is finding landlords who already participate — ask your PHA for its list and read our source-of-income protections guide.

Emergency Rental Assistance in Indiana

  • Dial 211 to reach local emergency rental and prevention funds and your regional Coordinated Entry for shelter and rapid re-housing
  • Community Action Agencies administer crisis help and the Energy Assistance Program (EAP/LIHEAP) through IHCDA — see utility assistance programs
  • Township Trustees in Indiana provide emergency assistance for rent and utilities — a state-specific resource worth contacting in your township
  • The Salvation Army, Catholic Charities, and St. Vincent de Paul provide one-time help across the state

Indiana Tenant Law: Key Protections at a Glance

Quick Reference: Indiana (IN)

  • Source-of-income protection: none — and the state preempts cities from creating it (IC 36-1-3-8.5)
  • Rent control: none statewide and in no city
  • Security deposit limit: no state cap
  • Deposit return deadline: 45 days, itemized (IC 32-31-3-12 and -14)
  • Eviction notice (nonpayment): 10-day notice to pay or quit (IC 32-31-1-6)
  • Notice to end month-to-month: 30 days (IC 32-31-1-1)
  • Implied warranty of habitability: yes — landlords must keep units safe and functional
  • Self-help eviction: illegal (IC 32-31-5-6) — no lockouts or utility shutoffs

Security deposits

Indiana sets no cap on the deposit amount. After you move out and provide a forwarding address, the landlord must mail an itemized statement of any deductions and refund the balance within 45 days (IC 32-31-3-12 and -14). If the landlord misses the 45-day deadline, you may recover the entire deposit plus reasonable attorney’s fees. See how to recover your security deposit.

Eviction process & how long it takes

Self-help eviction — lockouts, removing belongings, shutting off utilities — is illegal under IC 32-31-5-6, and the landlord must go through court. The sequence:

  • 10-day notice to pay or quit for nonpayment (other grounds use the notice in your lease)
  • Eviction (possession) action filed in court; you are served and given a hearing date
  • Possession hearing — appear and raise defenses; a separate hearing may address money damages
  • Order for possession: if the landlord wins, the court issues the order and the sheriff carries out the eviction

An uncontested Indiana eviction commonly runs about three to six weeks from the notice. Appear at your hearing with your lease, receipts, and photos. Get help at indianalegalhelp.org and read how to avoid eviction.

Where to Get Help in Indiana

Free legal aid: Indiana Legal Services (1-844-243-8570) handles eviction defense, habitability, and discrimination.

Fair housing: the Fair Housing Center of Central Indiana ((317) 644-0673) investigates discrimination complaints.

State housing agency: IHCDA for vouchers, LIHTC, and energy assistance.

Find your local PHA: HUD’s PHA directory or our how to find your PHA guide.

211 helpline: dial 2-1-1 or visit 211.org for rental help, shelters, and utility assistance.

HUD fair housing: file at hud.gov/reporthousingdiscrimination or call 1-800-669-9777.
